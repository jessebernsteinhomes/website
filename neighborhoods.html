Westside Neighborhoods

The Lay of the Land

Every Block Has a Story

The Los Angeles Westside isn't one neighborhood — it's a constellation of distinct communities, each with its own pace, character, and real estate personality. Jesse knows them all, block by block, and helps clients find the one that fits their life.

Neighborhood 01

Venice Beach

Venice Beach is unlike anywhere else on earth. The canals, the walkstreets, the murals, the muscle beach, the Abbot Kinney boutiques — it's a neighborhood that has always attracted the creative, the unconventional, and the visionary. Real estate here reflects that energy: architectural homes, converted lofts, canal-front compounds, and bungalows that have been transformed by designers with extraordinary taste.

The Venice Canals Historic District alone is one of the most coveted micro-markets in all of Southern California. Jesse knows which bridges to cross, which streets to target, and which properties hold the greatest long-term value.

Neighborhood 02

Marina del Rey

California's largest man-made small-craft harbor, Marina del Rey is a waterfront community that blends resort-style living with easy coastal access. Condominiums with marina slips, townhomes overlooking the main channel, and luxury high-rises with panoramic bay views make this one of the Westside's most diverse real estate markets.

For investors, Marina del Rey offers strong rental demand, short-term rental potential, and consistent appreciation driven by its proximity to Venice, LAX, Playa Vista's tech corridor, and Santa Monica's amenities.

Neighborhood 03

Santa Monica

Santa Monica sits at the northern edge of Jesse's core market and brings a more urban, polished character to the coastline. Montana Avenue, Third Street Promenade, Main Street — each corridor offers a distinct flavor, from boutique-lined streets to the iconic pier. Real estate ranges from entry-level condos to multi-million-dollar estates perched above the Pacific.

It's a city unto itself — with world-class schools, Michelin-starred restaurants, beachside yoga studios, and arguably the best farmers' market in California. The demand here is perpetual.

Neighborhood 04

Pacific Palisades

Nestled between the Santa Monica Mountains and the Pacific, Pacific Palisades is Westside living at its most serene and prestigious. Large lots, canyon estates, and cliffside homes define its character — a neighborhood where privacy, nature, and coastal beauty converge.

The Palisades attracts executives, entertainers, and families who want the best of Los Angeles without the noise. It is consistently ranked among the most desirable — and valuable — communities in Southern California.

Neighborhood 05

Mar Vista

Mar Vista is the Westside's most accessible and beloved residential neighborhood. Bounded by Venice, Santa Monica, Culver City, and the ocean, it offers exceptional value, tree-lined streets, and a real neighborhood feel that keeps residents for decades. Craftsman bungalows, Spanish revivals, and mid-century homes make up a housing stock that rewards both first-time buyers and seasoned investors.

The farmers' market on Sundays, Venice Boulevard, and proximity to Silicon Beach's tech employers make this one of the most searched zip codes on the Westside.

Neighborhood 06

Playa del Rey

Playa del Rey is Los Angeles's best-kept coastal secret. Bluff-top streets, wide uncrowded beaches, a small-town spirit, and some of the most stunning sunsets on the California coast. It sits between Marina del Rey and El Segundo, largely bypassed by the tourist trail — which is exactly why its residents love it.

With values notably lower than Venice or Santa Monica and a location that anchors LAX, tech, and the beach, Playa del Rey presents genuine upside for buyers willing to look where others haven't.
